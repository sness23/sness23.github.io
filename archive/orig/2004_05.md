<h3 class="title">trees</h3>

<p>Some types of tree datastructures</p>

<ol>
<li><a href="http://searchdatabase.techtarget.com/sDefinition/0,,sid13_gci509290,00.html">binary-tree</a>
A binary tree is a method of placing and locating files (called records or keys) in a database, especially when all the data is known to be in random access memory (RAM). The algorithm finds data by repeatedly dividing the number of ultimately accessible records in half until only one remains.
<img alt="binarytree.gif" src="http://www.sness.net/weblog/archives/binarytree.gif" width="178" height="217" border="0" />
</li>

<p><li><a href="http://searchdatabase.techtarget.com/sDefinition/0,,sid13_gci509290,00.html">B-tree</a><br />
A B-tree is a method of placing and locating files (called records or keys) in a database. (The meaning of the letter B has not been explicitly defined.). The maximum number of children per node is the order of the tree. The number of required disk accesses is the depth.<br />
<img alt="btree.gif" src="http://www.sness.net/weblog/archives/btree.gif" width="179" height="215" border="0" /><br />
</li></p>

<p><li><a href="http://searchdatabase.techtarget.com/sDefinition/0,,sid13_gci509290,00.html">quad tree</a><br />
A quad tree is a method of placing and locating files (called records or keys) in a database. The algorithm finds data by repeatedly dividing the number of ultimately accessible records in four parts until only one remains.<br />
<img alt="quad_tree.gif" src="http://www.sness.net/weblog/archives/quad_tree.gif" width="269" height="187" border="0" /><br />
</li></p>

<p><li><a href="http://searchdatabase.techtarget.com/sDefinition/0,,sid13_gci509290,00.html">splay tree</a><br />
A splay tree is a self-adjusting search algorithm for placing and locating files (called records or keys) in a database. The algorithm finds data by repeatedly making choices at decision points called nodes.<br />
</li></p>

<h3 class="title">exploring mars</h3>

<p><a href="http://marsrovers.jpl.nasa.gov/gallery/press/spirit/20040506a.html"><img alt="08-AK-01-traverse-map-B093R1_br.jpg" src="http://www.sness.net/weblog/archives/08-AK-01-traverse-map-B093R1_br.jpg" width="500" height="375" border="0" /></a><br />
This map illustrates the Mars Exploration Rover Spirit's position as of sol 112 (April 26, 2004), near the crater called "Missoula." Like a train on a tight schedule, Spirit will make regular stops along the way to its ultimate destination, the "Columbia Hills." At each stop, or "station," the rover will briefly analyze the area's rocks and soils. Each tick mark on the rover's route represents one sol's worth of travel, or about 60 to 70 meters (200 to 230 feet). Rover planners estimate that Spirit will reach the hills around mid-June. Presently, the rover is stopped at a site called "Plains Station." </p>

<p>The color thermal data show how well different surface features hold onto heat. Red indicates warmth; blue indicates coolness. Areas with higher temperatures are more likely to be rocky, as rocks absorb heat. Lower temperatures denote small particles and fewer rocks. During its traverse, Spirit will document the causes of these temperature variations. </p>

<p>The map comprises data from the camera on NASA's Mars Global Surveyor orbiter and the thermal emission imaging system on NASA's Mars Odyssey orbiter.</p>

<p>Adverse Effects</p>

<p>The consequences of high doses or long-term use of licorice are<br />
severe. This herb can cause high blood pressure, low levels of<br />
potassium, fluid retention and swelling of the face and limbs,<br />
hormonal imbalance, and muscle destruction leading to pain and<br />
weakness.</p>

<p>At least one woman experienced loss of libido. Another woman ate<br />
too much licorice candy and lost a great deal of potassium; her<br />
heart stopped.</p>

<p>Licorice can also change heart rhythms, prolonging QT and PR<br />
intervals on an electrocardiogram.</p>

<p>Lethargy and fatigue as well as weakness are part of the picture of<br />
licorice toxicity.</p>

<p>Many of the negative symptoms associated with licorice are due to<br />
its ability to inhibit the renin-angiotensin system. Elderly people in<br />
particular are susceptible to kidney problems as a consequence of<br />
licorice.</p>

<p>Paralysis of the legs (and in one case, of all of the limbs) has been<br />
reported. A sixty-four-year-old man developed pulmonary edema,<br />
signaled by fatigue and trouble breathing, after eating four<br />
packages of black Twizzlers licorice candy in three days. This<br />
case demonstrates how quickly a serious reaction can arise.<br />
Licorice can reduce thyroid gland activity and lower the basal<br />
metabolic rate.</p>

<p>It appears that individuals vary considerably in their susceptibility to<br />
adverse reactions from licorice. Some people experience negative<br />
symptoms within days, while others may ingest excessive licorice<br />
in candy, chewing gum, chewing tobacco, or herbal medicine for<br />
months or even years before they realize that they are suffering<br />
damaging effects.</p>

<p>Women appear to be somewhat more susceptible than men, and<br />
oral contraceptives may increase this sensitivity.</p>
